Two days is enough to understand why Kagoshima is different. Not to see everything — the prefecture takes weeks — but to feel the specific character of the place: the volcano, the bay, the food, the particular unhurried pace that southern Japan maintains against the Tokyo norm.

This itinerary assumes you arrive by shinkansen at Kagoshima-Chuo Station with two nights to spend.

---

## Day 1: The city and the volcano

### Morning — Sengan-en

Start at Sengan-en, the 17th-century samurai garden on the northern shore of Kinko Bay. Take bus route 1 or 2 from Kagoshima-Chuo (30 minutes). The garden opens at 8:30 AM; arrive early before the tour groups.

Spend two hours here. Walk slowly — the garden rewards it. The Shuseikan Museum is included in admission and worth 30 minutes for the industrial history context. The Shimazu family's role in Japan's Meiji modernization is a story most guidebooks underplay.

After the garden, stop at the Iso Onsen footbath outside the main gate.

### Lunch — Kagoshima City

Take the bus back toward the city. Get off at Prefectural Government Mae and walk to **Tenmonkan** — the covered shopping arcade district that functions as the city's social center.

For lunch, look for a tonkatsu specialist or a kurobuta ramen shop in the lanes around the arcade. Budget 1,000–1,500 yen.

### Afternoon — Sakurajima

Walk or tram to the **Sakurajima Ferry Terminal** (tram stop: Sakurajima-Torii-Mae). Take the 15-minute ferry to the island.

On the island, take the local bus or a rental bicycle to the **Arimura Lava Observatory** — the hardened 1914 lava field is one of the stranger landscapes in Japan. Return to the **Yunohira Observatory** for the view down toward the city and bay.

The **Nagisa Lava Trail** (3 km coastal path) can fill the remaining afternoon if the light is good.

Return by ferry in time for sunset over the bay.

### Dinner — Tenmonkan

Return to the city. Dinner at one of the kurobuta shabu-shabu specialists in Tenmonkan — **Kumasotei** is the reliable choice. Budget 4,000–6,000 yen per person with drinks.

After dinner, the Tenmonkan area stays lively until late. This is where to find the local izakayas and the best selection of imo shochu by the glass.

## Day 2: Ibusuki

### Morning — Ibusuki no Tamatebako

Take the 8:56 AM **Ibusuki no Tamatebako** scenic train from Kagoshima-Chuo (book reserved seats in advance — JR Pass accepted). Arrive in Ibusuki at 9:56 AM.

### Late morning — Sunamushi

Walk 15 minutes from the station to **Sunamushi Kaikan Saraku**, or take the shuttle bus. The sand bath experience requires about 45 minutes total (changing, bathing, showering). Go once before lunch while the bay light is at its best — you'll be looking at Kinko Bay from beneath the sand.

### Lunch — Ibusuki

Eat near the sand bath facility or back toward the station. Fresh seafood is the correct choice on the peninsula: grilled fish set meals (teishoku) are available at several small restaurants near the shore.

### Afternoon — Ikeda Lake or Cape Nagasakibana

**Ikeda Lake** is a 10-minute taxi ride north. The caldera lake is calm and large; cycle hire is available at the shore. The giant eels are real.

**Cape Nagasakibana** is the southern tip of the peninsula — 20 minutes by taxi. On a clear day, Yakushima appears on the horizon. The lighthouse and rocky shore reward an hour.

### Return

Take the late-afternoon Ibusuki no Tamatebako back to Kagoshima-Chuo. The return journey often has better light for the mountain views to the east.

## Logistics

**Accommodation:** Stay in central Kagoshima City (Tenmonkan area or near Kagoshima-Chuo). Avoid tourist hotels around the station — the business hotels one block further are cheaper and equally convenient.

**IC card:** Essential. Loads at any station machine. Covers trams, buses, and the Sakurajima ferry.

**Tamatebako booking:** Reserve online at JR Kyushu's website or at the station ticket counter. The scenic car fills up. A day in advance is usually sufficient except for Golden Week and summer weekends.

**Budget (rough):** Excluding accommodation — Day 1: ¥8,000–12,000. Day 2: ¥6,000–9,000.
