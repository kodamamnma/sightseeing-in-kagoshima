Sengan-en was built in 1658 by Shimazu Mitsuhisa, the 19th lord of the Satsuma domain, on the northern shore of Kagoshima's Kinko Bay. The garden uses Sakurajima and the bay as shakkei — borrowed landscape — treating the volcano and the sea as an extension of the garden design.

This is one of the great examples of the technique in Japan. The garden is beautiful in its own right; with Sakurajima perfectly positioned in the background of almost every view, it becomes something else.

## The garden

The main garden covers about 15,000 square meters. Paths wind through bamboo groves, plum orchards, stone lanterns, and carefully composed views. The Nanten-ryu (Southern Heaven) style of garden design is on full display — structured but not rigid, with views that reward slow movement.

Look for the **Nekoishi** (cat rock) — a naturally occurring volcanic stone formation said to resemble a sleeping cat. It's been a garden feature since the Shimazu era.

The **Moon-viewing Stone Platform** was built specifically for autumn moon-viewing over the bay. If you're visiting around the harvest moon (September/October), this is where to be.

## The Shokokan Museum

The Shimazu family still owns and operates Sengan-en. Their history is also the history of Meiji-era industrialization — the Satsuma domain led Japan's first experiments with Western manufacturing techniques in the 1850s and 1860s.

The Shokokan Museum on the grounds covers this history, including the Shuseikan, Japan's first modern factory, which now houses exhibits on Satsuma cut glass (kiriko) and the industrial heritage of the domain.

The Shuseikan complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site as part of the "Sites of Japan's Meiji Industrial Revolution" designation.

## Satsuma kiriko

The glass-cutting tradition developed here in the 19th century produces the distinctive red-over-clear geometric patterns seen in high-end souvenir shops across Kagoshima. The craftspeople at Sengan-en do demonstrations; pieces are expensive and worth it as a serious craft object.

## Practical

Sengan-en is 30 minutes north of the city center by bus (routes 1, 2, or 35 from Kagoshima-Chuo) or a 5-minute taxi ride from Iso Onsen. The bus stop is right at the gate.

Open daily. Admission covers the garden and Shokokan; the Shuseikan museum is a separate ticket.

Combine with a visit to Iso Onsen — the hot spring hotel adjacent to the garden — for a full day.
