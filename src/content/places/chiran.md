Chiran sits inland on the Satsuma Peninsula, surrounded by tea fields, about 45 minutes south of Kagoshima City. It contains two things that have nothing in common except geography: one of Japan's finest preserved samurai districts, and the country's most significant memorial to the tokko — the young pilots of the World War II special attack units.

Plan a full day.

## The Samurai Street (Bukeyashiki-dori)

Seven samurai residences along a 700-meter stone-walled lane have been designated National Historic Sites. The exteriors are intact from the Edo period; six gardens are open to the public. Each garden uses the surrounding landscape differently — hills, hedges, stone arrangements — all drawing on the same karesansui (dry landscape) principles applied to modest but carefully designed plots.

These aren't showpiece temples. They're the domestic gardens of a provincial military class, maintained with genuine care, and largely uncrowded outside of spring and Golden Week.

Entrance is a combined ticket for all open gardens. Budget two hours to walk slowly.

**Best in April:** The cherry blossoms along the lane and in several of the gardens are exceptional.

## Chiran Tokko Heiwa Kaikan

The Peace Museum for Kamikaze Pilots documents the tokko operations launched from Chiran Airfield in the final year of the Pacific War. Over 1,000 young pilots — most in their late teens and early twenties, many university students — flew their last missions from this base.

The museum holds letters, diaries, photographs, and personal effects left behind by the pilots before departure. A Zero fighter plane is displayed in the main hall.

It is one of the most affecting museums in Japan. The letters, translated into English, are the weight of it — not political objects, but the private writing of young people aware they would not return.

Allow two hours. This is not a museum to rush.

## Getting there

From Kagoshima City: take the Ibusuki no Tamatebako or a local train to Ibusuki and connect by bus (60 minutes), or take a direct highway bus from Kagoshima-Chuo (about 90 minutes). A rental car makes the Satsuma Peninsula — Ibusuki, Chiran, Cape Nagasakibana — into a manageable day circuit.
