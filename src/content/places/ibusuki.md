Ibusuki is 50 kilometers south of Kagoshima City at the tip of the Satsuma Peninsula, looking out across Kinko Bay toward Sakurajima. It's worth the hour-long train ride entirely for sunamushi — one of the strangest and most restorative experiences Japan offers.

## Sunamushi: the sand bath

Geothermal heat warms the black volcanic sand of Ibusuki's beach to around 50–55°C. Attendants at Sunamushi Kaikan Saraku — the main facility — provide yukatas (cotton robes), then bury you in the sand up to the neck. Ten minutes is the standard duration. The weight and heat combine to produce something between a sauna and a deep massage.

It's bizarre, it works, and it immediately becomes something you tell everyone about.

**Practical:** The main facility is Sunamushi Kaikan Saraku, a short walk from Ibusuki Station. It's open daily except for maintenance closures. Bring or rent a yukata. The outdoor sand bath (yugahama) faces the bay; there's also a covered indoor option.

## Getting there

The **Ibusuki no Tamatebako** (lit. "Ibusuki's Jewel Box") is a scenic limited-express train running from Kagoshima-Chuo Station to Ibusuki in about 55 minutes. The train is designed to evoke the mythological Urashima Taro, with one side of the carriage black and the other white. Reserved seats only — book in advance and it's covered by the JR Pass.

Local trains also make the run in about 90 minutes without reservation requirements.

## Exploring the peninsula

**Ikeda Lake** is a large caldera lake north of Ibusuki known for its population of large eels and (allegedly) a lake monster called Issie. A pleasant detour with cycle rental available nearby.

**Cape Nagasakibana** marks the southernmost point of the Satsuma Peninsula — a short drive or bus ride from Ibusuki, with clear weather views to Yakushima. The cape's lighthouse and the rocky shore make for a satisfying endpoint.

**Chiran** (45 minutes north by bus or car) houses the Chiran Tokko Heiwa Kaikan — the Peace Museum for Kamikaze Pilots — and a beautifully preserved samurai street. See the Chiran entry for details.

## Where to stay

Ibusuki has a concentration of hot spring resort hotels (onsen ryokan) along the bay. Most offer sunamushi as part of the package. A night here rather than a day trip extends the experience considerably — late afternoon sand baths and kaiseki dinner are a combination worth planning around.
