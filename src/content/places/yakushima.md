Yakushima is about 60 kilometers south of Kagoshima City, and it belongs to a different world. The island receives more rain than almost anywhere in Japan — some parts of the interior see 10 meters annually — which has produced a forest of impossible density. Moss covers everything. The light filters green even on clear days. The cedars at the island's heart have been growing for between 1,000 and 7,200 years.

This is the forest that Hayao Miyazaki's team studied when designing Princess Mononoke. If you've seen the film, walking Yakushima will feel like stepping into it.

## Getting there

**By ferry:** Toppy and Rocket high-speed ferries from Kagoshima Taniyama Port take 75 minutes to Miyanoura. Multiple daily departures. Book in advance — the ferries fill up.

**By plane:** Japan Air Commuter (JAC) flies from Kagoshima Airport in 35 minutes. Very small aircraft, very scenic approach.

Once on the island, rent a car at the port. Bus service exists but is infrequent and time-consuming for reaching the main trailheads.

## The trails

**Shiratani Unsuikyo Ravine** is the most accessible ancient cedar forest and the most direct Mononoke experience. A 3–4 hour circuit covers the main moss-carpeted valley and the iconic Yayoi Sugi cedar (approximately 3,000 years old). The trail is well-maintained. Go on a weekday if possible — the ravine draws significant visitor numbers.

**Jomon Sugi** is the oldest cedar on the island, estimated at 2,170–7,200 years. Reaching it requires a 10-hour round-trip hike or a 5-hour walk from the Arakawa trailhead (which requires a shuttle bus from Yakusugi Land). This is a serious undertaking. Prepare for rain — it will rain, regardless of forecast. The hike is worth it.

**Yakusugi Land** offers shorter circular trails (30 minutes to 150 minutes) through old-growth forest. Good for those not doing the full Jomon Sugi day.

## When to go

**Best:** May (before typhoon season, lush greenery), October–November (less rain, comfortable temperatures, good trail conditions).

**Avoid:** July–September (typhoon season, trails can close). Golden Week (heavily crowded).

## Practical notes

Accommodation in Yakushima is limited and books up far in advance for busy periods. Most visitors stay in Miyanoura or Anbo on the coast.

Bring full rain gear regardless of the forecast. The island makes its own weather.

The ancient forest areas are managed carefully — stay on marked trails, pack out all waste.
